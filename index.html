Research

Multimodal hopelessness detection · Mar – Dec 2025

Machine Intelligence and Security Research Lab, Tarleton State

My research question was simple to state and hard to answer: can a model detect hopelessness better when it sees both the words and an image, instead of either one alone? Turns out yes, by a lot.

  • Fusing CLIP text–image embeddings with logistic regression hit a 94.77% F1-score — 15.6 points over the image-only MobileNetV2 baseline. I benchmarked it against BLIP and across Random Forest and XGBoost too.
  • There was no dataset for this, so I built one: 3,882 text–image pairs extending the HopeEDI benchmark, with images from locally hosted Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, and hand-picked selection (80.62% inter-rater Kappa).
  • The work became a Springer book chapter (under peer review, expected 2026) and a poster at the RIED Research Symposium.

Purple Team Agentic Pipeline · Jan – May 2026

Cybersecurity capstone · team lead, 3 people

We built a RAG chatbot the way a company would have to: defense in depth, department data silos, compliance mapping — and then we attacked it ourselves, on a loop, until it stopped losing.

  • Final result: a 0% exploit rate across automated OWASP Top 10 LLM testing — prompt injection, PII leakage, document extraction, DAN jailbreaks, latent injection — behind dual-stage NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails.
  • The stack: Gemini API, Docker, OpenWebUI, and a hardened FastAPI gateway with ChromaDB attribute-based access control enforcing silos across 35 documents, 6 datasets, and 2 access tiers, aligned to NIST AI RMF and ISO 42001.
  • The red side ran itself: Garak, Giskard, and Promptfoo orchestrated through n8n, with Ollama generating adversarial prompts, feeding findings back into the hardening cycle. Arize Phoenix traced every request for incident-response audits.

GesturePass · Feb – Apr 2026

Machine learning course project · face gesture lead

Two-factor access control where both factors are gestures: one from your face, one from your hand. I owned the face side.

  • Six facial gestures (neutral, smile, left/right wink, mouth open, eyebrows raised) classified in real time from a webcam, using a MediaPipe FaceMesh pipeline and a 100-tree Random Forest.
  • The fun engineering problem: making it work no matter where you sit. I normalized 3D landmarks to the nose tip and scaled by inter-ocular distance, then killed prediction flicker with confidence thresholding.
  • A shared feature-extraction module guaranteed the training pipeline and the live demo computed features identically — no train/inference drift.
